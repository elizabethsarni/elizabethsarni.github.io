little kid's rock

This non-profit case study was done in an effort to increase engagement across the Little Kid’s Rock website. The goal was to not only improve application submissions but to also fully enlighten teachers on how to get involved and encourage engagement amongst other site visitors looking to donate. Along with that came a website redesign; the team worked towards cleaning up content, they simplified the navigation to the necessary pages, and incorporated the brand colors consistently throughout. The rebrand was created in an effort to modernize and bring a cohesive look to the overall organization.

YEAR: 2018
SERVICES: REBRAND, UX, CASE STUDY
*Liz is not affiliated with Little Kid's Rock

THE PROBLEM

CURRENT STATE

The team observed that the website isn’t engaging new teachers enough to help them learn about the services and complete/locate the process for applications. This is causing confusion to the end user and lowering impact of the organization. How might the team improve the Little Kids Rock site based on increased teacher application completions and increased Modern Band music classes in public schools across the United States.

THE SOLUTION

INSIGHT

A motivated music teacher needs a more simple way to find resources and curriculum so he can communicate with school boards and keep music alive in local schools and communities.

After gaining insight in to the needs of our user and pinpointing the design goals, the team began developing initial wireframes. The wireframes focused on the homepage, the application process and the confirmation page. Other pages for consideration included About Us and Learn More.

DELIVER

FEATURES

The new and improved homepage has implemented Google's material design stylesheet and guidelines. Along with that, features include a smooth scroll, carousel header, paralax scroll, updated CTA buttons and minimal content pods.

FUTURE CONSIDERATIONS

After rolling out the first round of improvements on the website and rebrand, the team will continue to iterate and has future considerations to map out. They consist of increasing responsiveness, implementing consistency in design, A/B testing and revisiting content clean-up.
